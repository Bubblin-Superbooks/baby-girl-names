Sonica and I love caucasian names but we also did not want our daughter's name to go by what those cheeky most popular suggestions on the Internet say.

We wanted to avoid as many confirmation biases as possible. Another aspect we noted about our searches was that we would often repeat a result from the shortlisted names. Take a suggestion from a friend, and run it through Google. Then go around in the family in a hope to incorporate a consensus.

Sometimes we would go in the direction of baking the family name or a cultural cue into the name, but it always turned out to be a little too much for us.

We knew our baby's name had to be simple. Short and stylish. It had to reflect the multi-culturalism our family reflects and speak to a secular way of life. Having scanned through as many names as possible, we landed on Zuvy for our baby which I hope our daughter will like when she grows up.

And so is born this book as well.
